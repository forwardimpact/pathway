# Spec 1210: Landmark evidence coverage too low to support Find Growth Areas

## Problem

A structured user-testing run of `fit-landmark` against the persona
`daedalus@bionova.example` (BioNova manufacturing IT data engineering manager,
J080) produced a coverage reading of 4.3% over a 180-day window. The session
findings are recorded in issue [#989](https://github.com/forwardimpact/monorepo/issues/989):

- `fit-landmark sources` returned 46 GitHub artifacts (PRs, commits, reviews).
- `fit-landmark evidence` returned 2 marker-evidence interpretations.
- `fit-landmark coverage` reported 4.3%.
- Both surviving interpretations carried `rationale: synthetic`.
- `fit-landmark timeline` surfaced two growth datapoints
  (`team_collaboration: foundational`, `cloud_platforms: working`).
- The persona's flagship recent work — a `mes-connector` v2 schema cutover
  across the manufacturing data fabric, and the `scada-bridge` operational
  record — did not appear as evidence on any surface.

The product-side cause is structural, not a content gap:

- `products/map/src/activity/transform/evidence.js:54-87` is the sole producer
  of marker-evidence rows. It reads `getdx/evidence.json` (a pre-authored
  external file), filters its rows by `person_email` match against
  `github_artifacts`, round-robin assigns each evidence row to artifacts
  whose author matches, and writes the resulting rows with
  `rationale: "synthetic"`. There is no producer that reads an artifact's
  title, diff, or review body and emits a marker-evidence interpretation;
  every interpretation in the database is either a pre-authored row from
  the GetDX file or a human-written row via the `WriteEvidence` RPC at
  `services/map/proto/map.proto:39`.
- `products/landmark/src/lib/evidence-helpers.js:114-120` computes
  `coverage = scored / total`, where `scored` is "artifacts joined to ≥1
  evidence row" and `total` is "all artifacts returned by `getArtifacts()`".
  With the only producer being a round-robin assignment off a small external
  file, the denominator grows with real GitHub activity but the numerator
  is capped at the size of the GetDX file's persona-matching subset. For
  this persona that ratio is 2/46.
- `rationale` is a free-text column on the `evidence` table (per
  `services/map/proto/map.proto:39`, "1–3 sentences" of human-written
  context). The only programmatic value ever written is the literal string
  `"synthetic"` at `products/map/src/activity/transform/evidence.js:81`,
  which is both the marker for "auto-generated row" and the marker for
  "delete-and-rebuild on next import" (line 20). The two roles are
  conflated; downstream code cannot distinguish "auto-generated by a real
  artifact interpreter" from "round-robin synthetic placeholder" from
  "human-attested."
- `fit-landmark coverage` and `fit-landmark readiness` are separate
  commands whose outputs are never adjacent. The readiness output
  (`products/landmark/src/formatters/readiness.js:26-32`) reports
  `X/Y markers evidenced` without surfacing the coverage ratio. A reader of
  a readiness verdict therefore cannot tell whether `X/Y` reflects the
  product's view of the persona's actual work or a measurement floor.
- Neither `evidence`, `timeline`, `readiness`, nor `coverage` produces
  observably distinct output when coverage falls below a confidence floor.
  All four surfaces report whatever rows exist as if the rows constituted
  a representative sample. For this persona the readiness verdict reads as
  "the work doesn't show much growth signal" — which the persona reports
  is materially indistinguishable from "the tool can't see your work."

## Persona and job

**Empowered Engineers → Find Growth Areas**
([JTBD.md:141](../../JTBD.md)). The Big Hire is "guidance and evidence
grounded in my organization's standard, not impressions or generic advice."
The Fired-When line reads "gap analysis disconnects from promotion
decisions; or a reorg makes the standard feel irrelevant." The persona's
own articulation captured during testing
([issue #989](https://github.com/forwardimpact/monorepo/issues/989)) names
the failure mode in those terms:

> "The 'broader influence / visible technical leadership' feedback from my
> manager and the 4% coverage from Landmark are saying the same thing, two
> ways. The work I'm proud of doesn't surface through the evidence chain.
> That's a real problem for me, but it's also a different problem than
> 'I'm not good enough at J080 yet.' I can move both, but not by doing more
> of the same."

The Competes-With line ("annual reviews; mentor conversations; hoping the
next project makes readiness obvious") is materially advantaged today: a
mentor conversation that names two pieces of work the persona is proud of
beats a tool that returns two synthetic interpretations of unrelated rows.

## Strategic position

The product owns two contracts: (1) the readiness verdict must reflect a
representative view of an engineer's recent work, and (2) when it cannot,
it must say so in terms the reader can act on. Today neither contract is
held: coverage degrades silently into the readiness verdict, and the
provenance of the rows that do exist is opaque. The fix has two leverage
points — increasing interpretation depth so the verdict has more rows to
reason over, and surfacing coverage adjacent to the verdict so the reader
can weigh it — and two mitigations — distinguishing rationale provenance
so synthetic rows are recognisable, and replacing the verdict with an
explicit negative-evidence framing when coverage is below a confidence
floor. Leverage points and mitigations are scoped together because
shipping only mitigations locks the product into "we're honest about not
being able to see your work," which materially worsens the Competes-With
position. Slicing the work is a design decision.

## Scope

| Surface | Change | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Activity-layer evidence producers under `products/map/src/activity/transform/` | introduce an artifact-driven interpretation path alongside the existing round-robin producer; the new path consumes GitHub artifacts (PRs, commits, reviews) and emits marker-evidence rows whose provenance is recognisably distinct from round-robin synthetic placeholders | grows the numerator of the coverage ratio for personas with real activity beyond the size of the persona-matching subset of `getdx/evidence.json` |
| Coexistence of the existing round-robin producer and the new artifact-driven producer on the `evidence` table | the resulting per-artifact evidence state converges on a single deterministic outcome regardless of producer ordering | repeated runs of the import path produce the same set of evidence rows whether the round-robin path runs first, the artifact-driven path runs first, or only one path runs |
| Provenance distinction on every evidence row | each evidence row, when consumed by landmark, can be classified into at least three provenance classes: round-robin synthetic placeholder, artifact-interpreted (programmatic), human-attested | downstream surfaces can label and (where required by criterion below) visibly differentiate output by provenance class; the storage mechanism — `rationale` prefix, a new column, a new proto field, or another carrier — is a design decision |
| `fit-landmark readiness` output (`products/landmark/src/formatters/readiness.js`) | surface the coverage ratio adjacent to the readiness verdict | a reader of a readiness verdict sees the measurement confidence in the same view |
| `fit-landmark readiness` and `fit-landmark timeline` output when coverage is below a documented confidence floor | produce observably distinct copy that frames the output as negative-evidence (the product cannot see enough of the engineer's work to deliver a verdict) rather than as a low verdict | when coverage is below the floor, the reader is told the floor was not met instead of being given a verdict that reads as a confidence statement |
| `fit-landmark coverage` output | label each interpretation by provenance class and show the breakdown alongside the ratio | a reader can see how much of the numerator is round-robin synthetic vs. artifact-interpreted vs. human-attested |
| `products/guide/starter/skills/evaluate-evidence/SKILL.md` step (e) | the `WriteEvidence` RPC call passes `provenance: 'agent_attested'` (alongside the existing `artifact_id`, `skill_id`, `level_id`, `marker_text`, `matched`, `rationale` arguments) so Guide's LLM-judged rows classify as `agent_attested` rather than falling through to the DB default (`human_attested`) | criterion 5's per-class breakdown distinguishes Guide-judged rows from hand-attested rows in the same deployment, without a second spec round |

### Out of scope

| Surface | Reason | Escape route |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Choosing the storage carrier for provenance class (existing `rationale` field, a new column, a new proto field, a metadata JSON column, etc.) | the spec contract is observable distinguishability of provenance class on each row; the carrier is a design decision bounded by criteria 2 + 5 | n/a — design picks the carrier |
| Replacing or removing `getdx/evidence.json` as a producer | the GetDX file remains useful as a baseline content seed and a fallback for personas with no GitHub activity; tightening or removing it is separable | follow-on spec if a second producer reaches parity coverage and the GetDX file's incremental value drops to zero |
| Backfilling existing `rationale: synthetic` rows to the new provenance vocabulary | the round-robin pipeline rebuilds those rows on every run (line 20 of the transform deletes them, line 81 re-inserts); next run after this spec lands writes the new value | n/a |
| Revising the 30% confidence-floor value once telemetry across real consumer installs is available | the spec hard-codes 30% as the initial floor (criteria 1 + 4); revising it once a coverage distribution exists is a separable change because the storage surface is already in place | follow-on issue once a coverage distribution exists across real consumer installs |
| Cross-marker calibration (e.g., weighting deep work vs. shallow PRs) | depth signals are an interpretation concern downstream of "the artifact was interpreted at all"; this spec gates on getting the artifact into the evidence pipeline | follow-on spec once the artifact-driven interpretation path lands |
| Changes to `fit-landmark sources` | `sources` already reports the denominator faithfully; the issue is at the interpretation and surfacing layer | n/a |

| --- | --- | --- |

| 2 | Every row in the `evidence` table exposes an identifiable provenance class drawn from at least three values: round-robin synthetic placeholder, artifact-interpreted, human-attested | reading evidence rows via the consumer-facing path (`fit-landmark evidence` against a seeded persona, in whatever serialised form the landmark surface returns evidence rows after this spec lands) yields a provenance value on every row that maps to one of the three documented classes |
| 3 | `fit-landmark readiness` output names the coverage ratio adjacent to the verdict, in the same screen the reader sees | run `npx fit-landmark readiness --email <seeded-persona>` and confirm the coverage ratio (numeric) appears in the same stdout block as the `X/Y markers evidenced` line, separated from it by no more than two text lines (no intervening blank section header or table) |
| 4 | A single product-facing confidence-floor threshold governs both criterion 1's ratio target and the negative-evidence framing on `fit-landmark readiness` and `fit-landmark timeline`; when measured coverage is below that threshold, both commands produce observably distinct copy that frames the output as negative-evidence rather than as a verdict | (a) confirm the floor value is named in one product-facing source of truth and that the same value is used by criterion 1's ratio target (currently ≥30%); (b) construct a seeded persona whose coverage falls below that threshold (e.g., a persona with the round-robin producer alone), run `npx fit-landmark readiness` and `npx fit-landmark timeline`, and confirm both stdout blocks include a discoverable string indicating the threshold was not met and naming what would change if it were |
| 5 | `fit-landmark coverage` output breaks down the numerator by provenance class | run `npx fit-landmark coverage --email <seeded-persona>` and confirm the output names each of the three provenance classes from criterion 2 with their respective counts (a class with zero rows is shown explicitly, not omitted) |
| 6 | Repeating the activity-import path produces the same set of evidence rows, modulo auto-populated timestamp columns | run `npx fit-map activity transform evidence` twice in succession against the same seeded `getdx/evidence.json` and the same set of `github_artifacts` rows; project the resulting `evidence` rows on (`artifact_id`, `skill_id`, `level_id`, `marker_text`, `matched`, provenance class) and confirm the set is equal between the two runs |
| 7 | Guide's `evaluate-evidence` skill writes evidence rows with `provenance: 'agent_attested'` | (a) inspect `products/guide/starter/skills/evaluate-evidence/SKILL.md` step (e) and confirm the `WriteEvidence` call signature names `provenance: 'agent_attested'` (no other call site in the file omits it); (b) execute the skill against a seeded persona with at least one scoreable artifact, then read the new evidence rows back via `getEvidence` and confirm every row inserted by this skill carries `provenance = 'agent_attested'` (not the DB default `human_attested`) |

## Risks

- **Numerator inflation via low-quality interpretations.** Adding an
  artifact-driven producer that emits many shallow rows would satisfy the
  ≥30% / ≥14 rows part of criterion 1 while leaving the underlying problem
  untouched. Criterion 1 part (c) anchors the verification on artifacts
  whose repository name matches the persona's team's repos (`mes-connector`,
  `scada-bridge`) rather than a bulk number alone, but the design must
  still take a position on what makes an interpretation load-bearing
  enough to be written; that position is a HOW decision.
- **The 30% confidence-floor is contestable.** Picking the initial floor
  without consumer telemetry is a guess. Criterion 4 ensures the same
  threshold governs both the ratio target and the negative-evidence
  framing, so a future revision moves both together. Design must document
  the path to revising the floor once coverage distributions across real
  consumers are available.
- **Criterion 5 introduces a new output element.** Today
  `products/landmark/src/formatters/coverage.js` breaks down by artifact
  type (PR / review / commit), not by provenance class. Criterion 5 adds
  a provenance breakdown alongside the ratio; existing type-breakdown
  output may coexist or be subsumed (design decision).
- **Provenance enum cardinality.** Criterion 2's three classes are a
  floor, not a ceiling. Design may propose additional classes if a forcing
  argument exists (e.g., sub-classes of artifact-interpreted by artifact
  type); a defensible ceiling is five classes — beyond that the
  user-facing breakdown in criterion 5 stops aiding the reader.
- **Existing test fixtures lock the current synthetic behavior.**
  `products/map/test/activity/transform-evidence.test.js` asserts
  `rationale === "synthetic"` on inserted rows in five test cases. Those
  fixtures must update in lockstep with the producer change; design
  records the migration shape and plan covers the file list.
- **Sibling-issue overlap with #985.** Issue #985 (data engineering /
  engineering management discipline coverage in the starter pathway) is
  also surfaced by the same persona's testing run. That gap independently
  blocks `Find Growth Areas` for non-SWE personas — even with the
  interpretation depth fix, a J080 persona reading against an SWE-only
  pathway would still miss discipline-aligned markers. This spec's
  criteria do not require #985's resolution. Issue #985 → Spec 1080
  (implemented in merged PR
  [#1062](https://github.com/forwardimpact/monorepo/pull/1062),
  2026-05-20).
