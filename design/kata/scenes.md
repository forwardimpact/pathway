# Kata Product Scenes

These scenes extend the
[reusable base scenes](../index.md#4-reusable-base-scenes) with Kata
production-floor symbols. All follow the
[scene grammar](../index.md#3-scene-grammar): 2px monochrome line art on a clean
white background.

For the Kata brand context — palette, typography, agent taxonomy — see
[index.md](index.md).

A note on the **Stakeholder's cap.** In Kata scenes the Stakeholder may wear a
soft flat cap (the Ohno cap) when on the shop floor — a brand-context accessory
layered over the family character's standard suit-and-tie. The cap does not
replace any inherited trait; it sits on top of "neat hair" the way a real cap
would. In scenes set off-floor (welcome, documentation), the cap may be absent.

## Scene: Storyboard Stand-up — The Hero Scene

**Context:** Kata internals page hero (`websites/fit/docs/internals/kata/`),
daily storyboard documentation, the default hero for any Kata-branded surface.

```
    ┌────────────────────────┐
    │ TO DO │ DOING │ DONE   │  ← storyboard
    │  ▢ ▢  │  ▢    │  ▢ ▢   │
    │  ▢    │       │  ▢     │
    └────────────────────────┘
       🐰      🤖       👔
       (reach) (point)  (cap, hands clasped)
```

Trio standing in front of a tall storyboard panel mounted on the wall — a
horizontal wire with kanban cards clipped on by clothes-pegs, three lanes
labelled To Do · Doing · Done. AI Agent (center) holds a pointer to a card on
the wire, head angled with mechanical attention. Engineer (left) is half-turned,
one hand already reaching for a card to move it — eager, moving before the
question finishes. Stakeholder (right, no backpack) wears cap and tie, hands
clasped behind back, leaning slightly forward — the foreman who has run this
meeting a thousand times. Shoulders close, clustered in front of the board.

**Key details:** Three speeds of work: Engineer already moving the card, Agent
still pointing at it, Stakeholder in no hurry to interrupt either. The
storyboard is the visual anchor — cards on a wire say "this is a working board,
not a slide deck." Stakeholder's cap is the brand mark: calm authority of
someone who has stood here every morning for years.

## Scene: Walking the Gemba

**Context:** "How a daily kata runs" page, agent profile pages, default brand
scene below the hero.

```
    🤖 ────→ 🐰 ────→ 👔
    📋        ↗       (cap, hands behind back)
    └─── floor markings ───┘
```

Trio walking the floor in single file along an implied line. AI Agent (front)
holds a clipboard at chest height. Engineer (middle) cranes to read over Agent's
shoulder — close enough to bump into them. Stakeholder (rear, no backpack) wears
cap and tie, hands behind back, gaze tracking outward toward the floor — not at
the clipboard. A few floor markings (taped lanes, station numbers) imply the
gemba beneath their feet.

**Key details:** The procession is the frame. Agent leads with the data,
Engineer chases the data, Stakeholder watches the work itself — the gemba walk
principle: the floor tells you more than the report. Three different focal
points (clipboard, screen, work) in one tight line.

## Scene: The Ohno Circle

**Context:** Improvement Coach product page, coaching session documentation,
kata-session skill page.

```
       🐰 (crouching, peering in)
        ↘
       ╭─────╮
       │  👔  │  ← Stakeholder in chalk circle
       ╰─────╯
              🤖📓 (recording)
```

A concrete factory floor seen from a low three-quarter angle. On the ground, a
small chalk circle — hand-drawn, wobbly, just wide enough for one person to
stand in, rendered in perspective as an ellipse flat on the floor surface.
Stakeholder (no backpack) stands inside the circle with both feet on the
concrete, cap pulled slightly forward, arms folded, feet planted — simply
observing. The chalk line passes around their feet at ground level. Engineer
crouches just outside the circle's edge, peering in with exaggerated curiosity,
ears tilted. AI Agent stands a respectful step further back, holding a notebook
open, recording. The circle is a floor marking like a taped workstation
boundary, not a floating ring or halo.

**Key details:** The circle is flat on the ground — drawn in chalk on concrete,
seen in perspective, not as a vertical shape or outline around a character.
Stakeholder's posture — relaxed, observing — is the entire coaching kata in one
frame. Engineer's curiosity is the audience surrogate ("why are they just
standing there?"). Agent's notebook captures everything Stakeholder is choosing
not to say.

## Scene: The Andon Cord

**Context:** Security Engineer product page, vulnerability response
documentation, "stop the line" coaching moments.

```
           ┃
           ┃  ← andon cord
           ┃
       🐰 ━┛
       (pulling)   🤖   👔  (cap, palm up)
                  (paused) "what did you see?"
```

A vertical cord hangs from above with a pull-handle at character height.
Engineer has both hands wrapped around the cord, leaning into it with full
weight — clearly _has_ pulled it. AI Agent stands beside them, one hand
half-raised — caught between approval and assessment. Stakeholder (right, no
backpack) steps in, cap slightly tilted, one hand raised in a calm "talk me
through it" gesture. The three stand close, clustered around the cord.

**Key details:** The frame is "stopping the line is a normal Tuesday." No alarm,
no panic. Engineer pulled because something looked wrong; Agent is already
cross-referencing; Stakeholder is treating it as a coaching moment. The cord is
the only vertical element — visually emphatic, like a plumb line.

## Scene: The Merge Gate

**Context:** Product Manager product page, issue and PR triage documentation,
agent-react workflow.

```
    🐰 ▢ ─── ▢ ─── 🤖[▢] ─── ▢ ─── 👔 │ →   ✓
    (pin new)    (examine)         (cap, stamp)│  past the gate
    ──────────────────────────────────────────┤
                                          gate post
```

A horizontal kanban wire runs left-to-right, ending in a small swing-gate with a
hanko stamp hanging from a string beside it. Cards clothes-pegged along the
wire. Engineer (left) pins a freshly written card, leaning in too close. AI
Agent (center) examines a card pinched between thumb and forefinger, head
tilted. Stakeholder (right, no backpack) stands at the gate, cap on, holding a
card up to read it — hanko stamp in the other hand, inkpad ready.

**Key details:** Three roles along one wire — intake, review, gate — but only
one of them stamps. The hanko in Stakeholder's hand is the scene's single warm
accent. Cards on a wire is unmistakably mid-century: kanban literally means
"signal card." The horizontal wire is the compositional spine — orderly,
left-to-right, terminating in a decision.

## Scene: The Drafting Bench

**Context:** Staff Engineer product page, spec → design → plan documentation,
plan execution flow.

```
        📐
       ┌────────────┐
       │ ▱ plan     │  ← drafting bench
       │ ──────     │
       │ ──── 🤖    │
       │ 🐰         │ 👔📝 (cap, glasses up, reading)
       └────────────┘
```

Trio gathered around a slanted drafting bench. A large sheet (the plan) is
weighted down with a set-square at one corner and a coffee cup at another. AI
Agent leans over the sheet, ruler in one hand, finger tracing a line. Engineer
stands at the foot, both hands planted on the lower edge, leaning forward.
Stakeholder (right, no backpack) is half-seated on a stool at the bench's end,
cap on, glasses pushed up, reading the spec annotations in the margin. Bodies
close around the bench.

**Key details:** The drafting bench is the workshop centerpiece — set-square,
sheet, coffee, marginalia. Three relationships to the plan: Agent measuring it,
Engineer ready to commit it, Stakeholder reading the why. The set-square corner
is the scene's icon-level anchor.

## Scene: The Shipping Bay

**Context:** Release Engineer product page, release readiness documentation.

```
                 📋 (manifest)
                 │
        ┌───────┐│  🤖🖊
        │ CRATE │
        │       │       👔🔴  (cap, stamp + ink)
        │   🐰  │
        └───────┘
       (closing lid)
```

A wooden crate sits center-frame, lid leaning beside it. A clipboard with a
manifest hangs on a nail to the right. Engineer crouches at the crate, both
hands on the lid, closing it with body weight. AI Agent stands beside the
manifest, pen poised over a checkbox, head turned toward the crate. Stakeholder
(right, no backpack) leans against the wall behind, cap on, holding a stamp and
inkpad — waiting for the manifest before stamping. The three are grouped tightly
around crate and manifest.

**Key details:** The frame is the relay — close, sign, stamp. Three hand-off
points, each one a checkpoint. The hanko stamp in Stakeholder's hand is the
scene's single warm accent — the only color on a monochrome page. That's the
brand motif, distilled.

## Scene: The Trace Tape

**Context:** Improvement Coach trace-analysis pages, `kata-trace` documentation,
grounded-theory coding flows, post-run review.

```
    🐰 (lifting fresh tape)
       ╲
        ▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒  ← tractor-feed tape
        │  │  │  │  │  │  │  │  │
        ↓                  ↓
        👔🔍               🤖📋
        (coding)          (tallying)
```

A long strip of tractor-feed printer paper unspools from a wall-mounted spool
and runs diagonally across the floor. The tape is marked at intervals with small
horizontal rules (turns) and tiny stamped icons (tool calls). Stakeholder (no
backpack) crouches beside the tape with a magnifying glass, cap on, eyes
following one specific line. AI Agent stands further along, holding a clipboard
with a tally sheet. Engineer is up on the spool side, lifting a fresh fold of
tape — keeping the read-head clear.

**Key details:** The tape is the visual anchor — trace data made physical, like
a 1960s mainframe printout. Three roles to the same record: the eye that reads,
the hand that codes, the helper that keeps the paper moving. The diagonal sweep
of the tape is the scene's distinguishing geometry — every other Kata scene is
composed horizontally; the trace cuts across.

## Scene: The Archivist's Desk

**Context:** Technical Writer product page, wiki curation documentation,
kata-documentation and kata-wiki-curate skill pages.

```
       👔  (cap, dictating)
       ──────────────
       │  manila folder  │
       │  ✒  ledger ─────│  ← desk
       │     🤖   📜🐰   │
       │     (writing)  (perched, reading upside-down)
       └─────────────────┘
```

A wooden desk with a manila folder open, a fountain pen, and a stack of weekly
logs. AI Agent sits at the desk, posture perfect, fountain pen in hand
mid-stroke, copying entries into a ledger. Engineer sits cross-legged on the
desk's right corner — perched where a chair should be — reading a log
upside-down. Stakeholder (no backpack) stands behind the desk, cap on, one hand
resting on the desk edge, dictating. The three are clustered around the desk's
working surface.

**Key details:** Three relationships to the written record: Agent transcribes it
precisely, Engineer reads it sideways (and somehow gets the gist), Stakeholder
dictates the canonical version. The fountain pen and manila folder anchor the
era — pre-keyboard, but disciplined.
